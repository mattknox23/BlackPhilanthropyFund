Leadership and Learning Forums (continued)

Objectives:

  1. Gain deeper understanding about the landscape of education reform in Boston and the Commonwealth in order to inform our investment strategy for improving the education of black and disadvantaged children.
  2. Outreach to a broader audience to build a learning and leadership community that works together to help make this happen.

The forums were designed to build shared understanding of the patterns of black children's achievement over time, how those patterns compare to the achievement of other racial groups in Boston and across the country; and, equally important, what works and what's needed to strengthen and accelerate the achievement of our children.

They were organized as panel discussions, and included:

  • Experts who know the data on education reform in Boston, the state, and the nation.
  • Scholars and practitioners knowledgeable about what is effective and needed regarding the education and development of black children.
  • Principals from improving schools already breaking new ground in achievement.
  • Funders and practitioners discussing what is needed to cut through the district vs. charter school gridlock.
  • State education leaders discussing the role of policy makers in addressing proficiency gaps among our youth.

Our forums were very successful. Each was attended by a diverse audience of 30-50 community members, including parents and community activists; educators, including teachers, principals and the school superintendents from Boston and Framingham; candidates in Boston's 2013 mayoral election; faith leaders, business leaders, and foundation executives; and education policy makers from Boston and the Commonwealth. These audiences were engaged, and they generated recommendations to help BPF develop a plan of action.
