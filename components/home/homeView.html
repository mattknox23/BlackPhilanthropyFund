Commitment

The Black Philanthropy Fund (BPF) is committed to making a difference in the educational achievement and opportunities of black and disadvantaged children. We regard ourselves as a learning and leadership community that works together, and engages others, in an ongoing process of learning and strategic action to achieve this goal.

The Boston Basics Campaign

The BPF's major initiative of 2015 has been the development and launching of the Boston Basics Campaign. The Campaign is a citywide effort aimed at equipping parents and caregivers of children aged 0-3 with a simple but effective set of five research-based tools that they can use to prepare their kids for high achievement in school. The Basics were developed by Dr. Ron Ferguson of Harvard's Achievement Gap Initiative (AGI). Research by the AGI shows that the achievement gap is firmly established before children even reach school. So, investing in improving the quality of our schools is important and necessary, but it is not sufficient to eliminate the achievement gap. The Boston Basics exploits this new understanding of the origins of achievement deficits; it focuses on the great opportunity engaged parents of infants and toddlers have by providing them with five simple, easy to use approaches to accelerating cognitive and emotional development. It's an affirmative investment, putting parents and caregivers in position to powerfully affect the foundations of learning and high achievement.
